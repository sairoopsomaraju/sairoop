Hello! My name is Sai Roop Somaraju, a Computer Engineering graduate student at Virginia Tech. My current research focuses on enhancing the eBPF subsystem within Linux kernels to improve safety, under the guidance of Dr. Dan Williams at the ROSA lab. Additionally, I'm contributing as a part-time assistant to Prof. Carl Harris, teaching an undergraduate course on Network Applications Design at VT.

Before joining Virginia Tech, I gained valuable industry experience as a firmware engineer at Qualcomm, Chennai, where I specialized in WLAN Hardware Abstraction and the PHY layer in the Firmware group. My career also includes a stint at a startup, where I developed edge computing solutions for IoT devices.

When I'm not engaged in programming, I enjoy backpacking in the woods, listening to music, and cooking.

If you're in search of an aspiring low-level developer with an interest in Systems Engineering, Firmware Engineering, or Network Engineering, feel free to email me at sairoop@vt.edu or connect with me on Linkedin.
